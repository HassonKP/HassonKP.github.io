About Me

My name is Killian Hasson, I am a United States Marine Corps veteran and recent graduate from Portland State University with a Bachelor of Science degree in Computer Science. I have been playing video games for as long as I can remember, quite literally one of my first memories was playing Super Mario Bros on the NES in 1999 and I haven't stopped since. At some point early in my life it became a dream to become a Marine like my father and so many others in my family; it felt like the natural course of action.

In 2013, the day after graduating High School I shipped off to MCRD San Diego, where I officially earned the title of United States Marine. After Basic Training I would attend School of Infantry aboard Camp Pendleton and eventually jump between a few different duty stations those being MCAS Miramar, Camp Lejeune and eventually finding myself back aboard Camp Pendleton. After a few years the opportunity to deploy with Special Purpose Marine Air Ground Task Force (SPMAGTF) 16 for Operation Inherent Resolve would appear and I would gratefully take it. While deployed I would earn my first Navy Achievement Medal and be faced with my biggest decision yet, staying in the Marine Corps or pursuing a new adventure.

In 2017, I separated from the Marine Corps and was unsure of the future, while reflecting on my past and what I wanted to do something occurred to me. I had never once stopped playing video games, even while deployed I spent 3 days on slow internet in the Morale, Welfare, and Recreation (MWR) tent downloading the latest World of Warcraft expansion. My first purchase with my first check in the Marines was for a Alienware 17, just so I could play Diablo 3 and everything else I happened upon. That's when I realized, Video Games were my passion, they always had been but school felt like a task I couldn't complete, but I had to try.

After years of school, countless failures, and twice as many accomplishments, I managed to acquire my degree. Here I am now, finally putting forth the things I have learned in college toward my true dream of breaking into the world of Game Development. A dream to shape and influence something that has been such a foundational part of my life, helping me to overcome everything from PTSD to depression and anxiety. As I move forward, I remind myself of a truth I’ve come to believe: Success isn’t measured in miles, but in inches. Every small step I take brings me closer to building a career and a life I can be proud of.
